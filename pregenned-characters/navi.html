Racial Traits (Na'vi)

Speed 35 ft. Darkvision 60 ft (bioluminescent markings; you see in darkness as dim light, dim light as bright light). Tsaheylu (Neural Bond): Advantage on Animal Handling checks. When you touch a creature with your neural queue (bonus action, willing or calm creatures only), you sense its emotional state — fear, calm, hunger, aggression — for 1 minute. Na'vi Resilience: Resistance to poison damage; advantage on saving throws against poison and disease. Languages: Na'vi, Common.

Class Features

Favored Enemy: Humans
Advantage on WIS (Survival) checks to track humans, and advantage on INT checks to recall information about them. You also know Common (already spoken). In the wider world, this applies to any humanoid enemies you become familiar with.
New player tip: Most dungeon humanoids are human — this gives you a tracking edge in wilderness scenes and free intel in encounters.
Natural Explorer: Forest
In forest terrain: double proficiency bonus on all INT and WIS checks; difficult terrain doesn't slow group travel; you can't become lost by nonmagical means; you remain alert while foraging and tracking; when traveling alone, you move stealthily at normal pace. Always move at full travel pace regardless of party size.
Fighting Style: Archery
+2 to attack rolls with ranged weapons. Already included in the longbow's +7 above (DEX +3, Prof +2, Archery +2). Doesn't apply to thrown weapons.

Beast Master: Animal Companion — Pa'li (Direhorse)Beast Master Conclave · DM approval for Large size
You have formed a tsaheylu bond with your pa'li, a six-legged Na'vi direhorse. It uses Riding Horse statistics (CR 1/4). The pa'li acts on your initiative. On your turn: bonus action to direct its movement; action to command it to Attack, Dash, Disengage, Dodge, or Help. If you give no command, it Dodges. If you are incapacitated, the pa'li acts independently on its own best judgment.

Pa'li stats: AC 10 · HP 13 (2d10+2) · Speed 60 ft · Large beast
STR 16 (+3) · DEX 10 (+0) · CON 12 (+1) · INT 2 (−4) · WIS 11 (+0) · CHA 7 (−2)
Hooves +5 to hit · 2d6+3 bludgeoning · reach 5 ft

While you ride the pa'li and it makes no attacks, you have advantage on DEX (Stealth) checks. If the pa'li dies, you can bond with a new beast after a 24-hour ritual in a natural setting.

Note: PHB Beast Master normally caps companion size at Medium. Using a Large pa'li is a common house rule for this character — confirm with your DM. As written, a Medium beast (e.g. a panther) is also legal.
New player tip: The pa'li is your mobility weapon. Ride it to close range on round 1, then Hunter's Mark + bow from horseback. Or command it to Help: that gives you advantage on your bow attack, turning every miss into a second chance. Keep it alive — 13 HP goes fast.

Once Per Long Rest · Character Quirk
Eywa Listens
You press your neural queue to any living natural surface — a root, the soil, a patch of grass, still water. For up to 1 minute, you feel the collective emotional state of the living things within 100 feet: calm, alarm, hunger, curiosity, grief, or wrongness. You cannot communicate or extract specific facts — only feel. The DM describes what the land feels, not what it knows. The DM may reveal that something dangerous passed through recently, that creatures are hidden nearby, or that the land itself is disturbed and afraid. Nothing in a stone dungeon can answer. A city park answers faintly. Old-growth forest answers clearly.
