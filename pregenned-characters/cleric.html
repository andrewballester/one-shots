Feat: War Caster

Advantage on CON saving throws to maintain concentration on spells. Can perform somatic components even with hands full. Can cast spells as opportunity attacks.

Class Features

Disciple of LifeLife Domain
Whenever you use a spell of 1st level or higher to restore hit points, the creature regains additional HP equal to 2 + the spell's level. So Healing Word (1st level) heals 1d4+3+3 = 1d4+6. Spiritual Weapon (2nd) adds +4 if it heals, but it doesn't — only healing spells apply.
Channel Divinity1 / Short or Long Rest
Two options:
Turn Undead: Each undead that can see or hear you within 30 ft must make a WIS save (DC 13) or be forced to flee for 1 minute.
Preserve Life: As an action, restore HP equal to five times your cleric level (15 HP) divided among any creatures within 30 ft that you can see. Each creature can be restored to at most half its HP maximum.
New player tip: Preserve Life is a free burst heal that doesn't use a spell slot — use it when two or three allies are hurt rather than burning a slot on one person.
Divine Strike
Not yet — this feature arrives at level 8. For now, you're a healer and blaster, not a melee damage dealer.

Cantrips (always available, no slot needed)
  • CantripSacred Flame — DEX save DC 13; 1d8 radiant; target gains no benefit from cover. Use on distant enemies or through walls.
  • CantripGuidance — Touch a willing creature; they add 1d4 to one ability check in the next minute. Excellent out of combat.
  • CantripToll the Dead — WIS save DC 13; 1d8 necrotic (1d12 if target is missing any HP). Better damage than Sacred Flame vs wounded targets.
Domain Spells (always prepared — never count against limit)
  • 1stBless — Concentration; up to 3 creatures add 1d4 to all attack rolls and saving throws for 1 min. The best buff in the game.
  • 1stCure Wounds — Touch; 1d8+3 HP healed. (Prefer Healing Word for the action economy.)
  • 2ndLesser Restoration — Touch; remove one disease, blindness, deafness, paralysis, or poison condition.
  • 2ndSpiritual Weapon — Bonus action; create a floating weapon (2d8+3 force) that attacks as your bonus action each turn. No concentration!
Prepared Spells (choose 6; rotate before each long rest)
  • 1stHealing Word — Bonus action; 1d4+6 healed (Disciple of Life). Best healing action-to-value ratio.
  • 1stGuiding Bolt — Ranged +5; 4d6 radiant; next attack vs target has advantage. Excellent for helping a striker land their hit.
  • 1stShield of Faith — Concentration; +2 AC to one creature for 10 min. Stack on your tankiest frontliner.
  • 1stInflict Wounds — Melee +5; 3d10 necrotic. High damage if you're willing to get in range.
  • 2ndAid — Boost max HP and current HP of up to 3 creatures by 5 each (8 hrs, no concentration).
  • 2ndPrayer of Healing — 10-minute ritual; up to 6 creatures regain 2d8+3 HP. Perfect post-combat patch-up (can't be used mid-fight).
Tip: Round 1: cast Bless (concentration) on yourself + two allies. Round 2+: Spiritual Weapon (bonus action) + Sacred Flame (action). You're buffing and attacking simultaneously without dropping Bless.
Once Per Long Rest · Character Quirk
Mild Prophecy
You pause, bow your head, and sincerely pray for guidance. Ask the DM one yes-or-no question about the current situation. The DM answers honestly, though the answer may be brief and may not specify which deity delivered it. This is not Commune — it's more like a gut feeling your god has occasionally confirmed to be accurate. It's also free. Aldric has come to rely on it more than he'd admit to anyone with theological training.
