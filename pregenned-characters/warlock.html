Feat: War Caster

Advantage on CON saves to maintain concentration spells. Can perform somatic components with weapons in hand. Can cast spells (like Eldritch Blast) as opportunity attacks.

Class Features

Pact Magic
You have 2 spell slots, both 2nd level. They recover on a short rest (10 minutes). Unlike other casters, every spell you cast uses 2nd-level slots — this makes all your spells slightly more powerful, but you have far fewer of them. Manage them like ammunition: use, short rest, reload.
New player tip: You are a cantrip-focused character who occasionally drops a big spell. Spam Eldritch Blast for free (no slot), then use your 2 slots for Hex or Hold Person. Short rest between every fight possible to recharge.
Eldritch Blast + Agonizing Blast
Eldritch Blast: your main attack cantrip — +5 to hit, 1d10 force at 120 ft. Free, infinite uses. Agonizing Blast (invocation): add your CHA modifier (+3) to the damage = 1d10+3 per blast. At level 3, one beam per attack action (two beams at level 5).
Devil's SightEldritch Invocation
You can see normally in magical and non-magical darkness to a range of 120 ft. This is dramatic: cast Darkness (2nd-level, from your spell list) on yourself, then shoot through it — enemies are blinded, you can see everything.
Pact of the ChainPact Boon
You have learned the Find Familiar spell and can cast it as a ritual. Your familiar can be an Imp, Pseudodragon, Quasit, or Sprite (special forms not available to wizards). You can see through your familiar's eyes and hear through its ears. When you take the Attack action, your familiar can also attack as a bonus action (using its own stats). You can deliver touch spells through your familiar.
New player tip: Keep the Imp in human form as your "advisor" NPC. In combat, order it to attack as your bonus action for extra damage. It can also turn invisible and scout.
Dark One's BlessingThe Fiend
When you reduce a hostile creature to 0 HP, you gain temporary HP equal to your CHA modifier + your warlock level = 3+3 = 6 temporary HP. These stack (to a max) if you keep killing things.

Once Per Long Rest · Character Quirk
The Imp Knows Things
You send the Imp familiar to eavesdrop on a conversation happening within 120 ft that you can observe. The Imp returns (in rat or raven form, ideally) and delivers a report: the general topic, one specific name that came up, and whether any money changed hands. The report is accurate. The Imp editorializes. Its opinions about the people it observed are delivered with confidence and are not always charitable. You've learned to request just the facts first, then ask for its thoughts as a separate step if you want them.
