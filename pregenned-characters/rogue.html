Racial Traits (Lightfoot Halfling)

Lucky: reroll any 1 on an attack, ability check, or save (must use new roll). Brave: advantage vs frightened. Halfling Nimbleness: move through creatures larger than you. Naturally Stealthy: can hide behind Medium or larger creatures.

Class Features

Sneak Attack2d6 · once per turn
You deal 2d6 extra damage on one attack per turn if either: (a) you have advantage on the attack roll, OR (b) a ally is within 5 ft of the target (and you don't have disadvantage). You don't need both conditions — just one.
New player tip: You almost never need to hide to get Sneak Attack. Just stay near a melee ally. Stand next to the fighter; they draw attention while you stab for +2d6 every round.
Cunning Action
As a bonus action, you can take the Dash, Disengage, or Hide action. This is how rogues survive — Disengage after attacking so enemies can't hit you back, or Hide to set up next round's Sneak Attack.
New player tip: Your basic combat loop: move in, attack (sneak attack), bonus action Disengage, move out. Or: bonus action Hide, move to better position, attack with advantage next turn.
Fast HandsThief Archetype
You can use Cunning Action to make a Sleight of Hand check, use thieves' tools to disarm a trap or open a lock, or use an object. Lets you pick a lock as a bonus action rather than an action.
Second-Story WorkThief Archetype
Climbing no longer costs extra movement. When you make a running jump, add your DEX modifier (+3) to the distance in feet. You can also climb sheer walls without an Athletics check in most cases.
Expertise (2 skills)
Stealth (+7) and Perception (+4) use double proficiency. Stealth +7 means you can hide reliably even against high passive perceptions.
Thieves' Cant
A secret mix of slang and signs used by criminals. Takes 4× as long to convey a message, but lets you communicate covertly with other criminals.
Once Per Long Rest · Character Quirk
Oh, This Old Thing
Pip produces any one mundane item worth 5 gp or less from somewhere on her person. Nobody knows how it got there. Pip does not know how it got there. It was definitely there all along. Valid examples: a key (unspecified), 10 ft of string, chalk, a lockpick, a candle stub, a whetstone, a folded piece of parchment (blank), a dead mouse (she doesn't know about this one either). One item per long rest. If the DM rules the item is too convenient for the exact situation, Pip finds it anyway and feels briefly smug about it.
