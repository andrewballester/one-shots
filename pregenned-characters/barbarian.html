Class Features

Rage3 / Long Rest
Bonus action to rage for up to 1 minute. While raging: +2 damage on STR-based melee attacks; advantage on STR checks and STR saves; resistance to bludgeoning, piercing, and slashing damage. Ends early if you take no damage and make no attack on your turn, or if you're knocked unconscious. Can't cast or concentrate on spells while raging.
New player tip: Rage almost every fight — resistance to physical damage means you effectively have double HP against swords and arrows. Use it as your first bonus action, then swing with your main action.
Unarmored Defense
While wearing no armour, AC = 10 + DEX mod + CON mod. You can still use a shield (AC 15 total for Grimtusk). Drop the shield when you want both hands on the greataxe (AC 13).
Reckless Attack
On your first attack of a turn, you can choose to attack recklessly: your attack rolls have advantage, but attack rolls against you have advantage until your next turn.
New player tip: Almost always worth it when you're raging (you'll tank the hits). Skip it if you're low HP and surrounded.
Danger Sense
You have advantage on DEX saves against effects you can see (traps, spells). Doesn't work if you're blinded, deafened, or incapacitated.
FrenzyPath of the Berserker
When you rage, you can go into frenzy. For the duration, you can make one additional melee weapon attack as a bonus action on each of your turns. When the rage ends, you suffer one level of exhaustion (disadvantage on checks; -1d4 removed per long rest). Frenzy means three attacks in a turn: Action + Action bonus weapon attack + Frenzy bonus attack. Use sparingly — exhaustion stacks.
New player tip: Save Frenzy for the boss fight. Regular Rage is almost always better since exhaustion hurts for the rest of the session.
Once Per Long Rest · Character Quirk
Primal Nose
As a free action, you sniff the air. You instantly know whether any living creatures are hiding or concealed within 60 ft — how many, and roughly what size they are (tiny, medium, large, etc.). You can't pinpoint exact locations, but you know they're there. Grimtusk describes this as a smell that can't be faked. It cannot be faked.
